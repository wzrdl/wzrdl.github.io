COVID-19 Infection Prediction using Physical Signs

Conference Paper 2022
Zirui Wen, Junjie Zhang, and Yuhao Zhang
International Conference on Cloud Computing, Performance Computing and Deep Learning (CCPCDL 2022)
Volume 12287 SPIE pp. 1-8

This paper presents a novel approach to predict COVID-19 infection using physical signs and symptoms. We developed a machine learning model that analyzes various physiological indicators to provide early detection capabilities. The model achieved 85% accuracy in predicting COVID-19 infection from physical signs alone, demonstrating the potential for non-invasive screening methods.

Bayesian Reinforcement Learning for Strategic Decision Making

Research Paper 2024
Zirui Wen, Research Team
Stevens Institute of Technology - AI Research Lab
In Progress Stevens Institute of Technology

This research explores the application of Bayesian reinforcement learning algorithms in strategic decision-making environments. We investigate how uncertainty quantification can improve decision quality in competitive scenarios, with particular focus on exploration-exploitation trade-offs and risk-aware policy optimization.

Large Language Model Optimization through RAG and Knowledge Graphs

Technical Report 2024
Zirui Wen, AI Research Group
Stevens Institute of Technology - Applied AI Research
Under Review Stevens Institute of Technology

This technical report presents our findings on enhancing Large Language Model performance through the integration of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and knowledge graphs. We demonstrate how combining structured knowledge with generative capabilities can improve accuracy, reduce hallucinations, and enhance contextual understanding in domain-specific applications.
